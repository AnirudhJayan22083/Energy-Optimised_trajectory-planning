Methodology

🗺 Trajectory Planning

Defines the motion path for the manipulator end-effector in both joint space and Cartesian space. Smooth, continuous trajectories reduce mechanical jerks and stress on joints.

  • Joint-space interpolation between waypoints
  • Velocity and acceleration profiles are constrained
  • Smooth paths reduce vibration at source
  • Forms the input to the Fourier shaping stage

Methodology

📐 Inverse Kinematics

Computes the required joint angles to achieve a desired end-effector position and orientation. Numerical IK is used as the unoptimized baseline.

  • Maps Cartesian goals → joint angle solutions
  • Numerical solver handles 4-DOF configuration
  • Baseline IK results collected for 4 test cases
  • Optimized algorithm compared directly against it

Methodology

〜 Fourier Transforms

FFT decomposes trajectory signals into frequency components. Fourier shaping modifies joint trajectories in the frequency domain to suppress dominant vibration frequencies.

  • Trajectory expressed as sum of sinusoidal components
  • Target suppression band: 4–7 Hz
  • Nonlinear sine terms make cost function non-quadratic
  • Modified trajectory transformed back via IFFT

Optimization Technique

Nelder–Mead Simplex Method

Derivative-free direct search — ideal for nonlinear, non-convex objectives. For 4-DOF, builds a simplex of 5 points and iteratively reshapes until convergence.

Why Not QP?

Quadratic Programming Violated

  • QP requires a strictly quadratic objective
  • Our cost includes nonlinear sine terms
  • Numerical velocity & acceleration gradients present
  • Adaptive scaling with clipping — not quadratic
  • Convexity cannot be guaranteed
  • Standard quadratic form cannot be written

Why Not ADMM?

Designed for Different Problems

  • ADMM targets large-scale convex problems
  • Requires separable problem structure
  • Our problem has only 4 variables — no benefit
  • Single nonlinear objective, not separable
  • Would add unnecessary complexity
  • No computational advantage here
